the basics

Currently I am a research scientist at the University of Colorado, Boulder and the Laboratory for Atmospheric and Space Physics (LASP). Over the years I have worked on numerous NASA sponsored projects that cross boundaries between climate and space sciences. In the most general sense, I am interested in the study of planetary atmospheres and their evolution. My primary tools are 3D general circulation models, line-by-line and correlated-k radiative transfer models, and cloud and aerosol microphysical models. I have researched topics including the evolution of Earth's climate through geologic history, the continuing co-evolution of the Earth-Sun system into the distany future, and the habitability of extrasolar planets. I am perhaps most known for developing and supporting ExoCAM, an extension of the NCAR Community Earth System Model for modeling non-Earth-like atmospheres.
