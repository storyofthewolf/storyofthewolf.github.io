the basics

Currently I am a research scientist at the University of Colorado, Boulder and the Laboratory for Atmospheric and Space Physics (LASP). Overpast decade I have worked on numerous NASA sponsored projects that cross boundaries between climate and space sciences. I have published research on topics including the evolution of Earth's climate through time, the Faint Young Sun Paradox, moist and runaway greenhouse climates for future Earth, the habitability of planets in other star systems, characterizing extrasolar planets through thermal emission, and the microphysical and optical properties of fractal aggregate aerosols. My primary tools are 3D general circulation models, line-by-line and correlated-k radiative transfer models, and cloud and aerosol microphysical models. I am perhaps most known for developing and supporting ExoCAM, an extension of the National Center for Atmspheric Research's Community Earth System Model that facilitates modeling of exotic (non-Earth-like) atmospheres. I have strong interestes in open-source software, building tools that are broadly useful to the community.

I am currently looking for new opportunities in science, industry, or elsewhere.
